# Difficult Flavor Combinations

Working notebook. Flavor combinations I dislike or find consistently disappointing. Some of these are popular pairings I just don't agree with; others are structurally hard to make work. Bullet list for now — each entry may grow its own section over time.

- **Mushrooms + cream + thyme.** The canonical european mushroom-cream sauce. Popular for a reason, but I find the thyme amplifies a funkiness in the mushroom-cream pairing that I'd rather suppress. Tarragon or rosemary tends to work better than thyme in this slot for me.
- **Meat + broccoli.** Common in american and chinese-american cooking (beef and broccoli, chicken and broccoli) but I find the combination consistently unsatisfying. Broccoli's sulfurous bottom notes fight rather than complement meat's savory ones, and the two textures don't integrate.
- **Pureed soups.** Velouté, bisques, smooth blended vegetable soups. Texturally one-note, and pureeing tends to flatten flavors that were more interesting when distinct. I'd rather eat a chunky stew or a clear broth than a smooth puree of either.
- **Indian coconut curry.** Indian cooking with a coconut milk base — common in southern indian dishes. Coconut works beautifully in thai cooking but in an indian context I find it dulls the spice profile rather than carrying it. Dairy (yogurt, cream, ghee) is the indian-curry fat I keep coming back to.
