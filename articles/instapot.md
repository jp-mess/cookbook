# Instapot Times

Working notebook. Recommended pressure-cook times for common ingredients, with stovetop alternatives where they exist. Logbook entries, not a manual.

## Rice

- **Instapot**: low pressure 3 min, then NPR 10 min.
- **Alternative**: stovetop from dry.

## Lentils

- **Instapot**: low pressure 5 min, then NPR.
- **Alternative**: soak overnight, then boil on the stove. Can't skip the soak if going stovetop — undersoaked lentils boiled from dry stay chalky.

## Beans (general)

- **Instapot**: high pressure 20 min from dry. Might not be enough.
- **If undercooked**: add 10-minute increments at high pressure and check between rounds. Watch for the ring blowing out — extended high-pressure cycles wear it down.

## Chickpeas

- Even harder than other beans — I've seen them take longer than 40 minutes at high pressure.
- I think they're better purchased canned. Extended high-pressure cooking is the main thing that blows out the silicone ring (a consumable on pressure cookers), and chickpeas are the worst offender.
- **Want to try**: an overnight soak first, to see if a 10-minute high-pressure cycle becomes viable after that. Would save the ring.
- **Canned isn't always done either**: even with canned chickpeas, you may have to boil them in baking soda for an extended time before they're soft enough for hummus and other dips. The skins need to break down further than canning leaves them.

## Chicken breast

- **Instapot**: 5 min high pressure, remove immediately.
- Produces great shreddable chicken.
- **Want to try**: low pressure for the same job. Haven't tested whether it makes a difference; the breast might prefer a gentler cycle.
